Immediate Help!
If you or someone you know is in immediate danger regarding mental health, it’s important to reach out for help right away. Please consider contacting a trusted friend, family member, or a mental health professional. If you feel unsafe, please call emergency services or a crisis hotline to provide immediate support. You don’t have to face this alone; there are people who care and can help you through it. Your safety and well-being are the top priority.

Get Help

Reach Out, Find Support

Speak To Your GP

For many of us, our local GP practice is the first place we go when we're unwell (known as primary care). Your doctor is there to help you with your mental health as well as your physical health. They could:

  • Make a diagnosis
  • Offer you support and treatments (such as talking therapies and medication)
  • Refer you to a mental health specialist, such as a psychiatrist
  • Recommend local support options.

Call A Crisis Hotline

Hotlines provide access to trained professionals who can offer immediate emotional support and guidance. Here are just some of the services available:

  • Samaritans
  • NHS 111
  • CALM - Campaign Against Living Miserably
  • Shout

Counselling

Counselling is a talking therapy that involves a trained therapist listening to you and helping you find ways to deal with emotional issues.

  • NHS Find a talking therapy service
  • Find a qualified therapist

Social Support

Supportive relationships provide a safe space to express feelings, which can help reduce feelings of loneliness and isolation. Friends and family can offer tangible assistance during difficult times, whether it’s help with daily tasks or navigating tough situations.
