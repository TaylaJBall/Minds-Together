Immediate Help!
If you or someone you know is in immediate danger regarding mental health, it’s important to reach out for help right away. Please consider contacting a trusted friend, family member, or a mental health professional. If you feel unsafe, please call emergency services or a crisis hotline to provide immediate support. You don’t have to face this alone; there are people who care and can help you through it. Your safety and well-being are the top priority.

Who Are We?

At Minds Together, we are dedicated to fostering mental well-being by providing accessible resources, support, and community for individuals navigating their mental health journeys. Our mission is to empower users with knowledge, reduce stigma, and promote open conversations about mental health. We strive to create a safe and inclusive space where everyone can find the information, tools, and support they need to thrive. Together, we can cultivate resilience, understanding, and hope for a healthier future.

It's Okay To Ask For Help

Asking for help with mental health is a strong and courageous step. Just like physical health, mental well-being can have its ups and downs, and it’s completely normal to seek support when things feel overwhelming. You deserve to feel supported and understood, and there’s no shame in prioritizing your mental health. Whether it’s through therapy, support groups, or just talking to someone you trust, taking that step can lead to meaningful changes and a brighter outlook.

Take 1 Day At A Time

Life can often feel overwhelming, especially when we think too far ahead. Embracing the idea of "one day at a time" can help ground us. Each day brings its own challenges and opportunities, and focusing on the present moment allows us to manage stress more effectively. Remember, it’s okay to have tough days. Allow yourself to feel your emotions without judgment. Surround yourself with supportive people and don’t hesitate to reach out for help.

Be Kind To Yourself

In a world that often demands so much, remember to take a step back and breathe. It’s okay to have tough days. Your feelings are valid, and it's important to treat yourself with the same compassion you offer to others. Celebrate your small victories, forgive your mistakes, and allow yourself to rest. You are doing your best, and that’s enough. Embrace your journey, and know that it’s perfectly okay to prioritize your well-being. You are worthy of love and kindness—especially from yourself.

You Are Not Alone

Stigma around mental health remains a significant barrier to seeking help, though awareness campaigns are gradually reducing stigma over time.

  • Around 1 in 6 children aged 5-16 years were identified as having a mental health disorder in 2021, which is an increase compared to previous years.
  • Approximately 1 in 4 adults in the UK will experience a mental health issue each year. This includes conditions like anxiety, depression, and more severe mental health disorders.
