Immediate Help!
If you or someone you know is in immediate danger regarding mental health, it’s important to reach out for help right away. Please consider contacting a trusted friend, family member, or a mental health professional. If you feel unsafe, please call emergency services or a crisis hotline to provide immediate support. You don’t have to face this alone; there are people who care and can help you through it. Your safety and well-being are the top priority.

Your Guide To Mental Wellness

Mindfulness

Mindfulness is a way of paying attention to the present moment, using techniques like meditation, breathing and yoga. It helps us become more aware of our thoughts and feelings so that, instead of being overwhelmed by them, we’re better able to manage them. Here are some tools to help you:

  • NHS Mental wellbeing audio guides
  • NHS How to meditate for beginners

Sleep Hygiene

Sleep hygiene is crucial because it sets the foundation for quality sleep, which is essential for overall health and well-being. By prioritizing sleep hygiene, you can significantly improve your quality of life! Here are some tools to help you:

  • NHS How to fall asleep faster and sleep better
  • Mind Tips to improve your sleep

Breathing Techniques

Breathing techniques help to lower your heart rate and blood pressure, promoting a sense of calm. Practicing these techniques regularly can help you to develop greater control over your emotions and become more adept at using them in moments of crisis. Here are some tools to help you:

  • Every Mind Matters Mindful Breathing Exercise Video
  • CHI Health Box Breathing for Stress Video
